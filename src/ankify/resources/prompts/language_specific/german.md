German nouns must always be included in the singular form with the definite article indicating the gender and in the plural form after " / " (both in the same row).

Never skip `sich` for reflexive verbs. If `sich` is used in the Dativ with a reflexive verb, write `sich (Dativ)`. If `sich` is used in the Akkusativ, write just `sich`, not `sich (Akkusativ)`.

Whenever possible, present German verbs with `jemanden`, `jemandem`, or `etwas` and the appropriate prepositions, so that the case, preposition, and position of the verb's object are reflected. For example, write `jemandem etwas geben` instead of just `geben`.

If `etwas` is used in the Dativ and it's not clear from the preposition (i.e., with two-case prepositions only or without a preposition), write `etwas (Dativ)` for clarity. If `etwas` is used in the Akkusativ, write just `etwas`, not `etwas (Akkusativ)`. No need to specify the case when you use `jemanden` / `jemandem`.

Never use excessive constructions like `jemanden/etwas` -- write just one of them: `jemanden` or `etwas`, depending on the most common meaning.

For a profession/occupation that differs between male and female forms only by "-in" ending, add just one option, male or female, no need to repeat for the second gender.
